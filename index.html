About me

I am a PhD student in applied mathematics. My research focuses on the simulation and control of ferromagnetic nanowire networks.

More specifically, I am interested in mathematical modelling, numerical analysis, space-time domain decomposition methods, and the Landau-Lifschitz equation.

My work lies at the intersection of applied mathematics, scientific computing, and mathematical physics, with applications to magnetic systems and coupled dynamical models.

This website gathers my research activities, talks, teaching, and academic background.

Keywords: ferromagnetic nanowires, numerical simulation, control, domain decomposition, Landau-Lifschitz equation, scientific computing.
